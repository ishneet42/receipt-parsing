%	ROW 1 LEFT — MOTIVATION

\headerbox{Motivation \& Approach}{name=motivation,column=0,row=0, span=2}{

Bill-splitting apps still require users to manually type every itemized amount.
A photographed-receipt pipeline that extracts each line item and price would close
that gap, but requires \emph{item-level} extraction — not the document-level fields
most receipt benchmarks emphasize.

\smallskip

\textbf{Two competing paradigms:}
\begin{itemize}\compresslist
\item \textbf{OCR-dependent} (LayoutLMv3): OCR engine $\rightarrow$ multimodal token
classifier. Risks \emph{error propagation} from OCR.
\item \textbf{OCR-free} (Donut): pixels $\rightarrow$ JSON, no OCR.
Risks \emph{hallucination} but bypasses OCR errors.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{We ask:} (1) Which paradigm wins on CORD? (2) How much of the score is the
model vs.\ the OCR? (3) Can we \emph{compose} them?
We propose \textbf{CHEF} (\textbf{C}omposed \textbf{H}ybrid for \textbf{E}xtraction \&
\textbf{F}ield-routing) — a field-routed composition that takes the winning model
per field.
}

%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
%	ROW 1 RIGHT — METHODS
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\headerbox{Methods}{name=methods,column=2,row=0, span=2}{

\begin{itemize}\compresslist
\item \textbf{LayoutLMv3} (Microsoft) — Tesseract v5 + multimodal transformer +
\textbf{BIO} token classification over 5 CORD fields. Fine-tuned by us on a MacBook
Air (MPS, frozen visual backbone, 8 epochs).
\item \textbf{Donut} (Naver) — Swin encoder + BART decoder generates JSON directly.
We use \texttt{naver-clova-ix/donut-base-finetuned-cord-v2}.
\item \textbf{CHEF (ours)} — line items $\leftarrow$ LayoutLMv3,
aggregates $\leftarrow$ Donut. Field routing only — no retraining.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Metrics:} entity-level F1, micro-F1, line-item matching accuracy, and a novel
\emph{checksum consistency} (does $\sum$items $=$ total $\pm 5\%$?). Tracked with
Weights \& Biases.
}

\headerbox{OCR-Source Ablation: How Much Was the OCR?}{name=ocr_ablation,column=2, below=methods, span=2}{

The 0.937 score uses CORD's \emph{human-verified} word boxes. Swap in real Tesseract
— same model, same images, same metrics:

\smallskip

\centering
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{lrr}
\toprule
\textbf{System} & \textbf{Micro F1} & \textbf{Line-item} \\
\midrule
LayoutLMv3 + \emph{gold} OCR        & \textbf{0.937} & \textbf{0.886} \\
\rowcolor{lossred}LayoutLMv3 + Tesseract & 0.314 & 0.061 \\
Donut (no OCR)                      & 0.878 & 0.732 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\smallskip

\raggedright
$\bullet$ Tesseract drops LayoutLMv3 by \textbf{0.62 micro-F1.} \\
$\bullet$ \textbf{${\sim}2/3$ of the headline score was the OCR}, not the model. \\
$\bullet$ With realistic OCR, \textbf{Donut wins by 0.564.} \\
$\bullet$ Standard CORD scores for OCR-dependent models = \textbf{upper bounds on deployable performance.}
}

%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
%	ROW 3 LEFT — ABLATIONS
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\headerbox{Ablations: Routing Direction \& Label Construction}{name=ablations,column=0, below=results, span=2}{

\begin{minipage}[t]{0.48\textwidth}
\textbf{Routing direction (negative control).}
We reverse CHEF: items $\leftarrow$ Donut, aggregates $\leftarrow$ LayoutLMv3.
If \emph{ensembling} alone caused the gain, reversed CHEF would also beat both.
It does not.

\smallskip
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lr}
\toprule
\textbf{System} & \textbf{Micro F1} \\
\midrule
LayoutLMv3 alone                  & 0.937 \\
Donut alone                       & 0.878 \\
\rowcolor{wingreen}\textbf{CHEF (ours)}       & \textbf{0.940} \\
\rowcolor{lossred}CHEF (reversed)             & 0.874 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\smallskip
\raggedright \footnotesize Routing direction \emph{itself} is the source of the gain,
not the act of combining models.
\end{minipage}
\hfill
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.48\textwidth}
\textbf{Label construction (\texttt{is\_key} fix).}
CORD marks key tokens (e.g.\ ``TOTAL'') with \texttt{is\_key=1}. Initially we labeled
keys and values identically — the model emitted ``\texttt{TOTAL 60.000}'' instead of
``\texttt{60.000}''. Filtering keys to background:

\smallskip
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lrr}
\toprule
\textbf{Variant} & \textbf{Micro} & \textbf{total F1} \\
\midrule
no \texttt{is\_key} filter       & 0.787 & 0.010 \\
\rowcolor{wingreen}with filter   & \textbf{0.937} & \textbf{0.898} \\
\midrule
$\Delta$ & \textbf{+0.150} & \textbf{+0.888} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\smallskip
\raggedright \footnotesize A 3-line change to the label pipeline accounts for
+0.150 absolute micro-F1.
\end{minipage}
}

\headerbox{Real-World Test \& Conclusions}{name=conclusions,column=2, below=ocr_ablation, span=2}{

\textbf{Real-world receipt photos.}
\begin{itemize}\compresslist
\item \texttt{bill.png} (Kroger): Tesseract reads ``TOTAL'' as \texttt{TOVAL};
LayoutLMv3 output is unusable. Donut recovers \texttt{KOROGER EGOS}, \texttt{ROMANE
TEATCUE}, etc.\ with plausible prices.
\item \texttt{bill2.png} (India Bazaar): CHEF extracts 5 prices
summing to \$34.71 — \emph{matching the predicted subtotal exactly.} Both models
agree on subtotal and total. \textbf{Cross-architecture agreement = a free
verification signal.}
\end{itemize}

\smallskip

\textbf{Checksum consistency.} Even CORD's own ground truth only passes
$\sum$items$=$total at $\sim$53\% (real receipts have tax/fees). All systems sit at
this ceiling, but \emph{mean absolute error} discriminates: LayoutLMv3 = 5.9\%,
Donut = \textbf{22{,}691\%} — Donut occasionally hallucinates totals four orders of
magnitude wrong, a failure invisible to F1.

\smallskip

\textbf{Conclusions:}
\begin{enumerate}\compresslist
\item Receipt parsing is a \emph{composite task}; line items reward token
classification, aggregates reward schema-constrained generation.
\item \textbf{CHEF is principled, not ad-hoc} — proven by the reversed-routing control.
\item OCR substitution reframes the literature: standard CORD scores
\textbf{overstate deployable OCR-dependent performance by $\sim 0.62$ F1.}
\end{enumerate}
